# Novelty Statement for StrawMind Project

This research addresses critical limitations in current smart agriculture AIoT solutions for straw mushroom cultivation, particularly in regions like the Mekong Delta. Our primary scientific contributions are:

1.  **Lightweight, Disease-Specific Object Detection Model for Edge AI:** We propose a novel, highly optimized StrawMind-YOLO model for accurate, real-time identification of specific straw mushroom diseases (Trichoderma spp., Aspergillus spp., Soft Rot). Engineered for resource-constrained edge devices (e.g., Raspberry Pi Zero 2W/RPi 4), this model demonstrates superior performance (balancing mAP and inference speed) compared to existing lightweight SOTA models, without compromising detection accuracy for subtle disease textures.

2.  **Comprehensive, Annotated Vietnamese Straw Mushroom Disease Dataset:** We curate and publicly release a new, high-quality dataset of straw mushroom images, meticulously annotated for the three target disease classes and healthy states. This dataset significantly expands upon existing limited and ambiguously labeled resources, incorporating diverse environmental conditions and growth stages relevant to Vietnamese mushroom cultivation, adhering to scientific standards (e.g., stratified splits, detailed metadata in `DATASET_CARD.md`).

3.  **AI-driven Closed-Loop Environmental Control in a Low-Cost AIoT System:** We develop and evaluate a novel AIoT system that integrates our lightweight disease detection model with environmental sensors and actuators. This system enables proactive disease management through automated microclimate adjustments, showcasing the practical feasibility and economic benefits for sustainable mushroom farming in developing regions.

These contributions collectively provide a robust, scientifically validated, and practically deployable AIoT solution for straw mushroom disease management, representing significant advancements over current practices and existing research.
